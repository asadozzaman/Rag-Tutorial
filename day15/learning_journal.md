# 15-Day Learning Journal

## Day 1

Learned the RAG mental model: retrieve first, then generate with grounded context.

## Day 2

Saw how chunking quality shapes retrieval quality before embeddings even matter.

## Day 3

Understood that embeddings should be benchmarked on real tasks, not only chosen by reputation.

## Day 4

Learned how indexing and hybrid search affect both latency and relevance.

## Day 5

Saw why reranking and retrieval strategy matter more than just increasing top-k.

## Day 6

Learned that prompt design controls faithfulness, structure, and failure behavior in RAG answers.

## Day 7

Understood framework-level abstractions like indices, query engines, and routing.

## Day 8

Learned to measure retrieval and generation quality instead of guessing.

## Day 9

Saw how query transformation and routing improve retrieval for messy user questions.

## Day 10

Learned when agents should decide between retrieval, tools, and calculation.

## Day 11

Understood how memory and history-aware reformulation make multi-turn RAG possible.

## Day 12

Learned to route between unstructured text and structured data like tables and SQL.

## Day 13

Saw what changes when RAG moves into production: caching, streaming, rate limits, and observability.

## Day 14

Learned advanced patterns for relationships, long documents, and self-correcting retrieval.

## Day 15

Combined all of the above into one end-to-end capstone system with ingestion, retrieval, memory, correction, evaluation, and deployment.

## Final Takeaway

Start simple. Measure everything. Add complexity only when the data and metrics justify it.
